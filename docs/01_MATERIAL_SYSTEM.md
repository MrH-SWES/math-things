# Math Things Material System

Math Things should feel more physical than ordinary digital UI because the objects are not actually tangible. Digital manipulatives must overperform tactility.

---

## Mathematical Material Objects

Colored mathematical pieces should read as **enameled cast iron**, like Le Creuset made base-ten blocks.

They should feel:

- dense
- heavy
- durable
- premium
- color-saturated
- rounded but not squishy
- glossy but not plastic
- manufactured rather than drawn

**They are not candy, jelly, flat UI tiles, toy plastic, or cheap classroom clip art.**

### Rendering details

- Rounded enamel edges
- Slight bevels
- Controlled specular highlights
- Heavy contact shadows
- Subtle darkening at seams and contact points
- Engraved or recessed unit divisions where useful

---

## Infrastructure Material

Infrastructure should read as **brushed or machined metal**:

- Beads
- Handles
- Rails
- Tracks
- Switches
- Cabinet bodies
- Drawer cases
- Axis guides
- Camera/control hardware

---

## The Bead

The bead is neutral brushed aluminum. It is a border-crossing object: origin marker, draggable point, cursor, decimal point, reset object, traveler, dropped number, center pivot, or object under investigation.

**The bead should never be assigned a place-value color.**

---

## Surfaces and Sets

Surfaces should feel premium, warm, and quiet:

- Warm showroom/workshop neutrals
- Soft light background
- Subtle glass or ceramic surfaces
- Brushed metal tracks
- White/off-white or satin graphite contrast where needed
- No clutter

The colored math objects should be the saturated elements. Infrastructure should support them rather than compete with them.

**Shorthand:** Le Creuset made the blocks; Snap-on built the cabinet; Apple shot the product video.

---

## Color and Structure

Color should reinforce structure, not decorate randomly.

Do not assign place-value colors arbitrarily. Do not add decorative color that competes with mathematical color.

---

## Place-Value Palette

Use the Math Things place-value palette consistently across all apps.

| Place | Hex Color |
|-------|-----------|
| Millions | `#FF00FF` |
| Hundred Thousands | `#6B3FA0` |
| Ten Thousands | `#FF7F00` |
| Thousands | `#4CBB17` |
| Hundreds | `#FFD700` |
| Tens | `#4169E1` |
| Ones | `#DC143C` |
| Tenths | `#64D2FF` |
| Hundredths | `#FF9F0A` |
| Thousandths | `#7B00FF` |
| The Bead | brushed aluminum / neutral gray |

Whole-number places grow leftward from ones. Decimal places shrink rightward from ones.
